Acorn Fairy Houses – Wireless Inductive LEDs

Someone once told me about a dreamland they created for their kids—a magical place where the lanterns only lit up when you were close to the ones you love. That beautiful idea stuck with me, and it became the inspiration for this project.

I started by designing small fairy houses, using acorns as roofs to give them a whimsical, woodland feel. To ground them in nature, I picked out a beautiful slab of walnut wood from Haverford College's arboretum, with the live edge bark still intact. I wanted this to feel like a tiny settlement, so I planned to add other elements like maybe a miniature campfire/ benches/ lampposts, and even a small body of water made from a resin pour.

But the idea that kept pulling me back was the glowing lanterns—lights that would only shine when someone you loved was nearby. After some research, I decided to incorporate wireless inductive LEDs, embedding induction coils in the wooden base and placing LEDs inside the huts. This way, the houses would emit a soft, warm glow when set down on the slab, bringing the dreamland to life.

My Process

1. I gathered all the electronic components and experimented with different wood thicknesses to ensure the inductive power transfer was strong enough to light up the LEDs. Once I found the right balance, I carved a cavity into the underside of the wood slab to embed the induction coils.

2. I sculpted the tiny houses out of clay and plaster, carefully embedding LEDs inside them. I also made a tiny campfire out of twigs, which ended up being one of my favorite details.

3. I carved out a cavity in the walnut slab for a resin pour to create the "sea". Then I finally poured the resin. This was the most nerve-wracking part. I messed up a couple of times, but after some trial and error, I got the effect I wanted.

4. The last step was sanding the walnut wood and treating it with Tried & True wood finish to bring out its rich, natural grain.

Reflections and the Result

This project was more than just a fun build—it was about capturing a feeling. The idea that light, warmth, and magic come alive in the presence of loved ones.
