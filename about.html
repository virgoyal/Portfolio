About Me

Hi, I'm Vir Goyal, a physics major at Haverford College and a master's student in Scientific Computing at the University of Pennsylvania. I’m passionate about pushing boundaries in research and engineering.

My work spans a diverse range of fields: I’ve researched axions as potential dark matter candidates, developed experimental setups to study stress patterns in photoelastic materials, and contributed to distributed temperature sensing in fiber optics for fire detection in naval aircraft carriers.

I’m also an experienced tinkerer and fabricator, having interned at Tsalla Aerospace, where I helped build and tune high-altitude drones. At Haverford’s Maker Arts Space, I’ve trained students in 3D printing, soldering, and CAD software. Whether it’s solving complex problems or mentoring others, I bring creativity, precision, and a collaborative spirit to all that I do.

Skills & Expertise

  • Proficient in CAD software (Fusion360, SolidWorks) and fabrication tools (3D printing, CNC machines, laser cutters).
  • Strong programming and analytical skills, with experience in Python, MATLAB, and statistical modeling.
  • Research experience in astrophysics, fiber optics, and materials science.
  • Leadership and mentorship through roles in academic and makerspace settings.
