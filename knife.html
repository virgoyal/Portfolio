Drop Point Hunting Knife

This was my first attempt at making a knife, and it was a hands-on learning experience filled with mistakes and creative problem-solving. I took a lot of inspiration from @WalterSorrellsBlades, whose work I really admire. If you’re into knife-making (and even if you aren't), I’d definitely recommend checking out his YouTube channel.


It all started with a slab 440c Stainless Steel. I sketched out the basic shape of the blade and handle I wanted onto the steel slab. The goal was to create a knife with a sleek yet functional design that could be used for various outdoor tasks.


After cutting out the rough shape with a hacksaw, I moved on to filing and sanding the edges. The process required patience and a lot of "eye-balling" to ensure the edges were smooth and symmetrical.


Next came the process of heat-treating the blade to increase its hardness and durability. Here I took the help of Peters' Heat Treating Inc. Once heat-treated, the blade was carefully polished and sharpened to achieve the desired finish. I started at 80 grit and worked my way up to 800 grit.







And here’s the final result: a handmade knife with a simple, sleek blade and a wooden handle. It wasn’t easy, but seeing it all come together in the end made the effort totally worth it.
