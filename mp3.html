DIY ESP32 MP3 Player

I love listening to music, but I never have enough storage on my phoneâ€”this is especially frustrating on long flights. So, I decided to take matters into my own hands and build my very own MP3 player!

This ESP32-based MP3 player is compact, portable, and super functional. It features a customized ESP32 board with an OLED display and an audio expansion shield based on the UDA1334ATS DAC chip. Together, they make it possible to play high-quality MP3 and WAV files from a microSD card. The player supports both headphones and speakers, thanks to a 3.5mm audio jack.

To make the device truly portable, it operates on a 5V power supply, so you can use a power bank or any USB power adapter. All the electronics are housed in a custom-designed 3D-printed case that I modeled and assembled to give it a clean, professional look.

I designed and laser cut a compact casing out of clear acrylic, ensuring portability while protecting the electronics. The artwork on the back was drawn by the talented Devika Bisht, and later laser etched onto a wooden panel, making this project as much about art as it is about the mechatronics.
